The following year they reach two to 3.5 feet tall and produce button-like clusters of small white flowers with four petals in the shape of a cross. How to Create a Track

Tracks are a way for you to share knowledge about the world around us.
Decide what to teach, then describe the track you’ll lay for people to follow.
Start walking a path or trail you know well. Stop and record your location when you’d like to share an observation or direct people’s attention.
On the trail or back at the office, you can add audio narrative, written description, and imagery to each location.
Add as many points as you like! When people pass by them, TrackJack will let them know there is something to learn.
